The Fisherman's Diary

Author/Director: Kang Quintus

Genre: Drama / Social Commentary

Year Release: 2020

Awards: Best Actor at New York International Film Awards (Kang Quintus)
Fun Fact: Based on true story of a young girl

The Fisherman's Diary follows a 12-year-old girl
in a conservative fishing village in Cameroon who defies tradition to pursue education.
Her journey challenges patriarchal norms and highlights the power of knowledge, resilience and gender equality.
Inspired by real events, this movie advocates for children's rights & social transformation in that society.


Character

  • Ekah
  • Teacher Bibih
  • Solomon
  • The headmaster

Memorable Scenes

  1. Ekah passionately challenges her father's refusal to let her attend school.
  2. Her words echo Malala Yousafzai's quote: “One child, one teacher, one book, one pen can change the world.”
  3. A haunting flashback reveals Solomon's traumatic past and why he fears education for his daughter.
